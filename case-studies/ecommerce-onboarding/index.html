Onboarding automation for an e-commerce brand

Industry: E-commerce / DTC · Engagement: [PLACEHOLDER: Q1 2026]

"They mapped our entire onboarding workflow in a single session and had an automation running within a week. We haven't touched it since."
— Director of Operations, E-commerce Brand

The challenge

A [PLACEHOLDER: ~$15M revenue] DTC brand had grown faster than its onboarding process. New customers hit a sequence of touchpoints — welcome email, account setup nudge, first-purchase offer, post-purchase confirmation, review request — but every one of those steps lived in a different tool, was triggered by a different person, and broke quietly during sale weekends.

The Director of Operations needed onboarding to be one workflow, not five, and to keep running on Black Friday weekend without anyone babysitting it.

Our approach

One working session in their office. We took a marker to a whiteboard, walked the existing onboarding step by step, and circled every place where the process depended on a human remembering to do something. There were [PLACEHOLDER: seven] of those.

From that one session we agreed on the target state — a single orchestrated workflow with a clear owner, every handoff explicit, and the failure modes monitored — and went away to build it.

What we built

  • A single onboarding orchestration that triggers off a new order in the e-commerce platform and runs every downstream step from there.
  • Connections into the brand's existing email, SMS, reviews, and helpdesk tools — no platform migration required.
  • Branching logic for first-time vs. returning customers, and for orders that include items requiring extra setup guidance.
  • A monitoring layer that watches for any onboarding step that fails or stalls, and pages the on-call operator before customers notice.
  • A simple weekly report showing onboarding completion rate, drop-off points, and time-from-purchase-to-first-engagement.

Results

  • 40% reduction in onboarding time from purchase to fully activated customer.
  • [PLACEHOLDER: ~95%] of new customers now complete every onboarding step, up from [PLACEHOLDER: ~70%].
  • Onboarding ran through [PLACEHOLDER: Black Friday / Cyber Monday] without manual intervention — handling [PLACEHOLDER: ~3x] normal daily volume.
  • Operations team eliminated a recurring [PLACEHOLDER: ~4 hour/week] manual handoff checklist.

Timeline

  • Day 1: Whiteboard session in their office, end-state agreed, scope locked.
  • Days 2–5: Build of the orchestration, integrations into the existing tool stack, branching logic for customer types.
  • Day 6: Side-by-side test against the manual workflow on a sample of real orders.
  • Day 7: Cutover. Monitoring live. Manual checklist retired.

Total elapsed time from kickoff to production: [PLACEHOLDER: one week].
